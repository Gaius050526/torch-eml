\begin{abstract}
Neural PDE solvers such as physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) can approximate
solutions to partial differential equations with high accuracy, yet they produce opaque
weight matrices rather than interpretable mathematical expressions.
We present a method for discovering closed-form symbolic solutions to PDEs using
compositions of verified elementary function primitives derived from the EML
(Exp-Minus-Log) function $\mathrm{eml}(x,y) = e^{x} - \ln y$, recently shown by
Odrzywo{\l}ek to constitute a universal function basis~\cite{odrzywołek2026}.
Rather than optimizing over raw EML expression trees---where even multiplication
requires a reverse-Polish representation of length~41---we construct verified
implementations of $\sin$, $\cos$, $\exp$, and $\ln$ from the base EML operation
via algebraic identities and treat these as differentiable building blocks.
Our \textsc{ComposeHead} architecture searches over trainable linear combinations
of separable products of axis-aligned primitives, reducing the optimization problem
from fitting arbitrary network weights to identifying a small set of symbolic
coefficients.
A post-training normalization and coefficient-snapping pipeline converts the learned
representation into exact symbolic form.
We demonstrate the approach on the two-dimensional Navier--Stokes equations, where
it recovers the Taylor--Green vortex solution
$u = \sin(x)\cos(y)\exp(-t/5)$,\;$v = -\cos(x)\sin(y)\exp(-t/5)$
at machine precision (pointwise error ${<}\,10^{-6}$), yielding a fully interpretable,
closed-form expression directly from data.
\end{abstract}
